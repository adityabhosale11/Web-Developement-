Biography of Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs is the authorized self-titled biography of American business magnate and Apple co-founder Steve Jobs. The book was written at the request of Jobs by Walter Isaacson, a former executive at CNN and Time who had previously written best-selling biographies of Benjamin Franklin and Albert Einstein.

Based on more than 40 interviews with Jobs conducted over two years—in addition to interviews with more than 100 family members, friends, and adversaries (opponents in a contest)—Isaacson was given unprecedented access to Jobs's life.

Jobs is said to have encouraged the people interviewed to speak honestly. Although Jobs cooperated with the book, he demanded total editorial control asked for no control over its content other than the book's cover. Isaacson commented that he had striven to take a balanced view of his subject that did not sugarcoat Jobs's flaws.

The book was released on October 24, 2011, by Simon & Schuster, 19 days after Jobs's death.

A film adaptation written by Aaron Sorkin and directed by Danny Boyle, starring Michael Fassbender, was released on October 9, 2015.
